Magnetosphere

Being one of the most interesting fields and regions in space, the magnetosphere never fails to evoke a sense of curiosity within the minds of astronomers and space freaks.

In a nutshell, the magnetosphere of a body is the region or space around a celestial body which is controlled by the body's magnetic field. To understand this, lets take an example of Earth itself.

Sun and Earth

The direct reason for the shape of Earth's magnetosphere is being blasted by solar winds. That very wind compresses the sunward side of Earth's magnetosphere to only 6-7 times the radius of earth.

Due to this, a supersonic shock wave is created in the sun-facing side of Earth, called the bow shock. Now, particles from the solar winds are slowed down and heated at reaching the bow shock. This causes them to perform roundabouts around the Earth in the Magnetosheath (The magnetosheath is a turbulent region permeated by large amplitude waves and hot particles that have been created in the shock formation). The night-side of the magnetosphere is most probably 1000 times the radius of Earth apart (there's no clarity, this is just the average estimated distance). The extension of the magnetosphere is called the magnetotail. And the outer boundary of Earth's geomagnetic field is called the Magnetopause.
