Black holes

Black holes are SO dense and have such an IMMENSE gravitational pull, such that even light cannot escape the pull.

The first body identified to be a black hole is Cygnus X-1,

Black holes, as we all know, have been one of the most mysterious bodies to have roamed the universe we know today, but times have changed, now we've developed new ways to track and capture black holes, (A photo of a black hole in the gallaxy M87 was taken recently!).

Types of Black holes

  • ▶ Suppermassive Black holes
  • ▶ Stellar Black holes
  • ▶ Intermediate blackholes
Each of these black holes are deadly and intresting in their own ways

Supermassive black holes are the ones which usually lie at the center of most galaxies (Saggitarius A* being the one for Milky Way). They're by far the largest type of black holes found These black holes are usually billions of times as massive as our Sun, but around the same size in diameter. Apparently, Supermassive Black holes can be a result of multiple tiny black holes which merge together, or large gas clouds collapsing together and rapidly accreting mass. There are many theories as to how Supermassive Black holes form, but these two theories seem the most accurate ones so far.

Stellar black holes are by far the smallest discovered type of black holes, but that doesn't make it less deadly. When a large star collapses, it continues to compress and eventually turns into a stellar black hole. These black holes are formed by individual stars, which make them incredibly dense. A stellar black hole packs the mass of more than 3 Suns into the diameter of JUST a city! This will lead to a unimaginable amount of gravitational force pulling on objects around the black hole, which also lets it dust and gas from its surrounding galaxies, keeping them growing in size.

“ “Eyes as black and as shiny as chips of obsidian stared back into his. They were eyes like black holes, letting nothing out, not even information.””

Intermediate black holes are lesser known black holes, as there isn't much clarity as to whether they exist or not. But recent research HAS revealed that it's highly possible that midsize, or intermediate black holes (IMBh is the short form*) could exist.

Such bodies have high chances of forming when stars in a cluster collide, in a chain reaction. There's also a high chance that many of these IMBhs collide together to form a Supermassive black hole.

Why so dense

Black holes are expected to form when a massive star dies. After the star's nuclear fuel is exhausted, its core collapses to the densest state of matter imaginable, a hundred times denser than an atomic nucleus.
