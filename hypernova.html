Hypernovas

Many of us wonder, whether there ACTUALLY is a difference between a supernova and a hypernova, fact is that the difference is far from being none

Hypernova, in a nutshell, is the gravitational collapse of a huge and massive star, eventually forming a black hole.

But there is an obvious similarity, both are explosions/self detonations of stars Coming to the differences, Hypernovas can my 5 to 50 times brighter than a supernova. Adding to that, supernovas often leave behind debris, or in some cases fade to invisibility as neutron stars, whereas hypernovas often leave behind black holes (as mentioned above)

How are gamma bursts inolved

Hypernovas also are often associated with the release of intense gamma-ray bursts

If hypernovas had to be quantified, they'd be as strong as stars of 500-100 solar masses when the explosion occurs, and when the balance between fusion and gravity is lost, gravity takes the front seat and due to the INCREDIBLE weight of the star's matter pushing themselves within, they eventually collapse into themselves, causing an INTENSE explosion, where all matter collapses into one singularity, which eventually forms the heart of a black hole (the singularity!).

“I think that we are like stars. Something happens to burst us open; but when we burst open and think we are dying; we're actually turning into a supernova. And then when we look at ourselves again, we see that we're suddenly more beautiful than we ever were before!”

Some of the stars' debris will be left orbiting the black hole, getting heated to over billions of degrees. All this energy will be concentrated at the poles of the black hole due to the strong magnetic field of the black hole.

Due to gamma-ray bursts, all this energy and matter travel to the nooks and corners of the universe
