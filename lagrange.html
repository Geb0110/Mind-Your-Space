Lagrange points

Lagrange points, it's not a name much heard of, but it's one of the most important and intriguing topics in the field of Space Science.

Lagrange points are points in space where objects tend to stay still. The science behind this is very thought-provoking.

Basically, Lagrange points are regions in space where the g-forces of a two body system like Earth and Sun produce enhanced and precise points of attraction and repulsion. This is often used by space probes to considerably reduce fuel consumption. These can be used by spacecraft as "parking spots" in space to remain in a fixed position with minimal fuel consumption.

Lets dive deeper

These points are special regions where the g-pull of two large masses precisely match with the centripetal force required for a small object to move with them.

When it comes to quantifying lagrange points, there are 5 known points, L1, L2, L3, L4 and L5. Out of these 5 points Two points are stable (L4, L5) and the other 3 are unstable (L1, L2, L3). The stable lagrange points (L4 and L5) form the apex of two equilateral triangles which have the large masses at the vertices.
