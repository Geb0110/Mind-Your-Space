Neutron Stars

At the end of a stars life, it explodes into a supernova, and if the star isn't that huge/massive, its core will

collapse, eventually ending up as a tiny and hyperdense body with nearly as same mass as our Sun. These hyperdense cores of detonated stars are now Neutron Stars, these are one of the most

juggernaut and bizarre objects in the universe we know today.

How much do these old geezers weigh?

If a Neutron Star had to be quantified, a typical one would have 1.4 times our sun's mass, but they can also range up to about 2 solar masses.

Surprisingly, in a Neutron Star, All the large mass (larger than/equal to our sun's) is squeezed into a sphere that's only about 15 km (10 miles), which is the average size of a city on Earth!

“Suppose you form a neutron star that is close to the upper limit. If enough stuff falls back, it'll push the star over this limit and a black hole will form - Deepto Chakrabarthy ”

This makes them EXTREMELY dense, but after a supernova explosion, the gravity continues to compress the Sun-sized core (The Neutron Star) to a point where the atoms become SO compacted and close, that electrons are violently thrusted into their parent nuclei, combining with protons to form neutrons, hence earning the name "Neutron Star"

So basically gravity in itself created a hyperdense, neutron-rich material called neutronium, which is a city sized cellestial beast!
